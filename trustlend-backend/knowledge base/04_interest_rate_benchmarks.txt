INDIA INTEREST RATE BENCHMARKS FOR LOAN FAIRNESS ASSESSMENT
=======================================================================

SECTION 1: RBI POLICY RATES (AS OF APRIL-MAY 2026)
-----------------------------------------------------
Repo Rate (RBI):                  5.25% per annum
Reverse Repo Rate:                Always lower than repo rate (approx. 4.75-5.00%)
Marginal Standing Facility (MSF): 5.50% per annum
Bank Rate:                        Higher than repo rate
Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR):        3.00% (cut cumulatively 100 bps through FY25-26)

NOTE: The RBI cut the repo rate by a cumulative 125 basis points across FY2025-26, bringing it from 6.50% (its level from Feb 2023 through Oct 2024) down to 5.25% as of April 2026.


SECTION 2: MCLR (MARGINAL COST OF FUNDS BASED LENDING RATE)
-------------------------------------------------------------
MCLR is the MINIMUM rate below which banks cannot lend (with certain exceptions).
Introduced by RBI in April 2016. Banks set MCLR based on their own cost of funds.
Most consumer loans (home, auto, personal) are priced at MCLR + spread.

TYPICAL MCLR RANGE (Public Sector Banks, 2024-2026):
- Overnight MCLR:     ~8.10% – 8.25%
- 1-Month MCLR:       ~8.20% – 8.40%
- 3-Month MCLR:       ~8.35% – 8.60%
- 6-Month MCLR:       ~8.65% – 8.90%
- 1-Year MCLR:        ~8.75% – 9.10%  ← MOST COMMON BENCHMARK FOR CONSUMER LOANS
- 2-Year MCLR:        ~8.90% – 9.10%
- 3-Year MCLR:        ~9.00% – 9.20%

SBI 1-Year MCLR (Oct 2024): 8.95%
SBI Base Rate (Sep 2024): 10.40%
SBI BPLR (Benchmark Prime Lending Rate): 15.15% p.a.

KEY POINT: The 1-year MCLR (~8.95% for SBI) is the benchmark most auto, home, and personal loans are priced against.


SECTION 3: EXTERNAL BENCHMARK LENDING RATE (EBLR)
---------------------------------------------------
Since October 2019, RBI mandated that floating rate loans to retail and MSME borrowers be linked to external benchmarks (not just MCLR).

Most common external benchmark: RBI Repo Rate.

EBLR Formula: Repo Rate + Bank's Spread
SBI EBLR (2024): 6.50% (repo) + 2.65% (spread) = 9.15%

Home loans linked to EBLR reprice within 1-3 months of RBI rate changes.


SECTION 4: TYPICAL INTEREST RATES BY LOAN TYPE (2024-2026)
------------------------------------------------------------

HOME LOANS:
- Typical range: 8.50% – 10.50% p.a.
- Best/prime borrowers: ~8.50% – 9.00%
- Average: ~9.00% – 9.65%
- HIGH RISK THRESHOLD: Above 12% for home loans is a red flag

PERSONAL LOANS (Unsecured):
- PSU Banks: 10.50% – 13.50% p.a.
- Private Banks: 11.00% – 15.00% p.a.
- NBFCs: 14.00% – 22.00% p.a.
- CONCERNING RANGE: 22% – 30% p.a. (borderline predatory)
- HIGH RISK THRESHOLD: Above 30% p.a. is predatory for most segments

AUTO / VEHICLE LOANS:
- Typical range: 9.50% – 12.00% p.a.
- CONCERNING RANGE: Above 15% for auto loans

EDUCATION LOANS:
- Typical range: 8.50% – 11.00% p.a.

MICROFINANCE / SMALL LOANS (NBFC-MFIs):
- RBI-regulated cap: ~26.5% (approximately — RBI sets margin cap)
- Typical: 20% – 26%
- Above cap is a compliance violation

GOLD LOANS:
- Typical range: 9.50% – 14.00% p.a.

MSME / BUSINESS LOANS:
- PSU Banks: 9.00% – 12.00% p.a.
- NBFCs: 12.00% – 24.00% p.a.

PAYDAY / SHORT-TERM LOANS (Digital):
- Often 24% – 50%+ p.a. (highly concerning)
- These frequently involve hidden charges that push effective rate higher


SECTION 5: INTEREST RATE FAIRNESS THRESHOLDS (RULE OF THUMB)
--------------------------------------------------------------
For a given loan type, the fairness assessment can use these bands:

EXCELLENT (Fair):
- Home loan: ≤ 9.50%
- Personal loan: ≤ 13%
- MSME loan: ≤ 12%

ACCEPTABLE:
- Home loan: 9.50% – 11%
- Personal loan: 13% – 18%
- MSME loan: 12% – 16%

CONCERNING (Needs Review):
- Home loan: 11% – 14%
- Personal loan: 18% – 24%
- MSME loan: 16% – 22%

HIGH RISK / POTENTIALLY UNFAIR:
- Home loan: > 14%
- Personal loan: > 24%
- MSME loan: > 22%

PREDATORY:
- ANY loan type: > 36% p.a. effective rate (APR including all charges)
- Hidden charges pushing effective rate above 40%+ p.a.


SECTION 6: FLOATING VS FIXED RATE RULES
-----------------------------------------
RBI mandates for floating rate retail/MSME loans (since Oct 2019):
- Must be linked to external benchmark (repo rate, MCLR, etc.)
- Cannot use internal benchmarks alone for these segments
- Rate changes must be clearly communicated to borrower
- Borrower has right to switch to fixed rate at reset date (with reasonable fee)

UNFAIR FLOATING RATE CLAUSES:
- "Rate may be revised at lender's discretion without notice"
- No mention of what benchmark the floating rate is linked to
- No mention of reset frequency
- No floor/cap mentioned for rate movement

=======================================================================
SCORING IMPLICATIONS FOR LOAN DOCUMENT ANALYSIS
=======================================================================

Use these benchmarks to calibrate interest rate fairness score:

PERSONAL LOAN SCORING:
- Rate ≤ 14%:   No deduction (fair range)
- Rate 14-20%:  Deduct 5 points (slightly high)
- Rate 20-28%:  Deduct 20 points (high)
- Rate > 28%:   Deduct 40 points (predatory range)

HOME LOAN SCORING:
- Rate ≤ 10%:   No deduction
- Rate 10-13%:  Deduct 10 points
- Rate 13-16%:  Deduct 20 points
- Rate > 16%:   Deduct 40 points

GENERAL UNSECURED LOAN / UNCLEAR TYPE:
- Rate ≤ 15%:   No deduction
- Rate 15-20%:  Deduct 10 points
- Rate 20-30%:  Deduct 25 points
- Rate > 30%:   Deduct 40 points

FLOATING RATE ISSUES:
- No benchmark specified: Deduct 15 points
- "Lender's discretion" language: Deduct 20 points
