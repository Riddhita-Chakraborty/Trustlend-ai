PREDATORY CLAUSE GLOSSARY & RISKY LOAN TERMS REFERENCE
=======================================================================

PART A: HIGH-RISK / PREDATORY CLAUSES (SEVERE — Flag Immediately)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

CLAUSE TYPE 1: MANDATORY ARBITRATION CLAUSE
Definition: A clause that requires the borrower to resolve ALL disputes through private arbitration rather than through courts or consumer forums.
Why Dangerous:
- Removes borrower's right to approach consumer court, civil court, or banking ombudsman
- Private arbitrators are often appointed or paid by the lender
- Arbitration is expensive and complex for small borrowers
- Class action suits become impossible
Identification phrases:
- "Any dispute shall be resolved exclusively through arbitration"
- "Borrower waives right to court proceedings"
- "Sole arbitrator appointed by the lender"
- "Disputes to be settled under Arbitration and Conciliation Act exclusively"
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 15 points


CLAUSE TYPE 2: WAIVER OF RIGHTS CLAUSE
Definition: Borrower gives up (waives) fundamental legal rights such as right to notice, right to object, right to legal recourse.
Why Dangerous:
- Borrower may unknowingly surrender ability to challenge unfair actions
- Can waive right to receive notice before collateral seizure
- Can waive right to dispute errors in account statements
Identification phrases:
- "Borrower hereby irrevocably waives..."
- "Borrower waives all defenses and objections"
- "Right to notice is hereby waived"
- "Borrower shall not raise any objection to..."
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 15-20 points


CLAUSE TYPE 3: DYNAMIC / VARIABLE INTEREST RATE WITHOUT BENCHMARK
Definition: Interest rate can change at the lender's discretion without being linked to a published benchmark.
Why Dangerous:
- Borrower has no way to predict or verify rate changes
- Rate can be increased arbitrarily
- Violates RBI EBLR mandate for retail/MSME floating rate loans
Identification phrases:
- "Interest rate shall be revised from time to time at the bank's discretion"
- "Rate of interest is subject to change without notice"
- "The applicable rate will be as determined by the lender"
- No mention of any benchmark (Repo rate, MCLR, etc.)
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 20 points


CLAUSE TYPE 4: BALLOON PAYMENT CLAUSE
Definition: Most EMIs are small, but a very large final payment (balloon) is due at the end.
Why Dangerous:
- Borrower may not realise how much the final payment is
- If unable to make balloon payment, all prior payments are lost
- Creates refinancing trap — borrower forced to take a new loan
Identification phrases:
- "Final instalment of Rs. X [significantly larger than regular EMI]"
- "Bullet payment due at end of term"
- "Residual value payment at end of tenure"
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 15 points (if undisclosed or disproportionate)


CLAUSE TYPE 5: CROSS-DEFAULT CLAUSE
Definition: Default on ANY other obligation (even unrelated) automatically triggers default on THIS loan.
Why Dangerous:
- A missed credit card payment can trigger home loan default
- Creates domino effect leading to property seizure
- Often very broadly worded
Identification phrases:
- "Default on any other obligation to any lender shall constitute default hereunder"
- "Cross-default provisions apply"
- "Event of default includes default under any other agreement"
Fairness Assessment: MODERATE-HIGH RISK — deduct 10-15 points


CLAUSE TYPE 6: ACCELERATION CLAUSE (UNILATERAL)
Definition: Lender can demand FULL IMMEDIATE repayment of entire outstanding loan at their sole discretion, without borrower fault.
Why Dangerous:
- Loan can be "called" by lender at any time
- Borrower has no protection against this
- Often triggered by lender discretion, not just borrower default
Identification phrases:
- "Lender may at its sole discretion call the entire outstanding immediately"
- "The loan shall become immediately due and payable upon lender's demand"
- "At lender's option, the entire loan becomes payable immediately"
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 15-20 points


PART B: MODERATE-RISK CLAUSES (Flag and Assess)
-------------------------------------------------

CLAUSE TYPE 7: EXCESSIVE PREPAYMENT PENALTY
Definition: A fee charged when borrower pays off loan before the scheduled end date.
Context: Some prepayment penalty is normal. It becomes problematic when:
- It applies throughout the loan tenure (no sunset period)
- The penalty is more than 2-3% of outstanding principal
- It applies even in the 4th, 5th year+ of the loan
- It prevents refinancing to a better rate loan
RBI Context: RBI has not banned prepayment charges entirely for all loans. For floating rate home loans and MSME loans: prepayment charges are prohibited per RBI rules.
Identification phrases:
- "Foreclosure charges of X% shall apply"
- "Pre-payment penalty of X% of outstanding amount"
- "Borrower shall not prepay without lender's prior consent"
Fairness Assessment: MODERATE RISK — deduct 5-10 points depending on rate and tenure
HIGH RISK if: Floating rate home/MSME loan with prepayment penalty — RBI violation


CLAUSE TYPE 8: INSURANCE BUNDLING (FORCED)
Definition: Lender mandates purchase of insurance (life, health, property) as condition of loan.
Context: Insurance is not inherently bad, but becomes a problem when:
- Premium is added to loan principal (you pay interest on insurance cost)
- Lender-affiliated insurer is specified (conflict of interest)
- Insurance cost is not included in APR/KFS calculation
- Multiple insurance products are forced
Identification phrases:
- "Borrower must purchase [lender-specified] insurance as condition of loan"
- "Insurance premium to be funded from loan proceeds"
- "Loan protected by [lender's subsidiary] insurance"
Fairness Assessment: MODERATE RISK — deduct 10-15 points if forced + not disclosed in APR


CLAUSE TYPE 9: NEGATIVE AMORTIZATION
Definition: Monthly payments are set lower than the interest that accrues, so the LOAN BALANCE GROWS even while making payments.
Why Dangerous:
- Borrower believes they are repaying but debt is actually increasing
- Balance can grow to exceed original loan amount
- Traps borrower in long-term debt spiral
Identification phrases:
- "Minimum payment option available"
- "Deferred interest plan"
- EMI amount that appears too low relative to loan size and tenor
Mathematical check: If monthly EMI × tenure < loan amount × 1.5, check if negative amortization may apply.
Fairness Assessment: HIGH RISK — deduct 25 points


CLAUSE TYPE 10: UNILATERAL MODIFICATION RIGHTS
Definition: Lender can change loan terms (fees, rates, conditions) without borrower consent.
Why Dangerous:
- After signing, terms can be changed adversely
- Contradicts RBI KFS rules (charges not in KFS cannot be levied)
Identification phrases:
- "Lender reserves the right to amend these terms at any time"
- "Terms and conditions may be revised at the bank's discretion"
- "Borrower shall be deemed to have accepted any such changes unless they object within X days"
Fairness Assessment: MODERATE RISK — deduct 10-15 points


PART C: HIDDEN CHARGE RED FLAGS
---------------------------------

PROCESSING FEES:
- Normal: 0.5% – 2% of loan amount (one-time)
- Concerning: Above 3% of loan amount
- Red flag: Not mentioned upfront; deducted from disbursed amount without disclosure

DOCUMENTATION CHARGES:
- Normal: Rs. 500 – Rs. 5,000 (flat)
- Red flag: % of loan amount; not disclosed in KFS

ACCOUNT MAINTENANCE CHARGES:
- Normal: Nil or small flat annual fee
- Red flag: Monthly charge % of outstanding balance

INSPECTION/MONITORING FEES:
- Normal: For secured business/property loans
- Red flag: Annual % of loan amount for simple personal loans

CONVERSION CHARGES:
- When floating rate borrower wants to switch to fixed: Some fee is normal
- Red flag: Very high conversion charge (>1% of outstanding) that prevents borrowers from exercising their rights

PART D: LEGALLY SIGNIFICANT RED PHRASES (Extract These from Documents)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The following phrases in any loan document should be immediately extracted and flagged:

- "At the lender's sole discretion"
- "Without notice to the borrower"
- "Irrevocably waives"
- "Including but not limited to" (in charge schedules — implies hidden charges)
- "As may be amended from time to time" (in rate/charge clauses)
- "Subject to applicable charges" (vague — what charges?)
- "Lender may demand immediate repayment"
- "Penal interest at X% per month" (old-style, now non-compliant post Jan 2024)
- "Interest compounded on default charges" (non-compliant post Jan 2024)
- "Sole arbitrator appointed by the bank"
- "Class action waived"
- "Governing law: [foreign jurisdiction]" (unusual in Indian retail loans)

SCORING GUIDE SUMMARY
=======================================================================

Score deductions:
Mandatory arbitration with no consumer forum recourse: -15
Waiver of fundamental borrower rights: -15 to -20
Floating rate with no benchmark disclosed: -20
Balloon payment (undisclosed): -15
Cross-default clause: -10 to -15
Unilateral acceleration: -15 to -20
Excessive prepayment penalty on floating rate loans: -10 to -15
Forced insurance not in APR: -10 to -15
Negative amortization terms: -25
Unilateral modification rights: -10 to -15
Old-style compounding penal interest: -15 to -25
Hidden processing fee > 3%: -10
No KFS / APR disclosure: -30
