RBI FAIR PRACTICES CODE FOR NBFCs & DIGITAL LENDING GUIDELINES
=======================================================================

SECTION 1: NBFC-SPECIFIC FAIR PRACTICES CODE
---------------------------------------------
Applicable to: All Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and Residuary Non-Banking Companies (RNBCs)

1.1 LOAN SANCTION LETTER
NBFCs must issue a loan sanction letter in vernacular language to the borrower that clearly states:
- Amount of loan sanctioned
- Annual interest rate applicable
- All applicable fees (processing, documentation, etc.)
- Method of application of interest (monthly reducing, annual, flat)
- Repayment schedule

RED FLAG: Loan documents that state "flat rate" interest instead of reducing balance rate are misleading. A 12% flat rate is equivalent to approximately 21-22% reducing balance rate.

1.2 FLAT RATE vs. REDUCING BALANCE — CRITICAL DISTINCTION
Flat Rate: Interest calculated on original principal throughout tenure.
Reducing Balance: Interest calculated only on outstanding principal.

Example:
Loan: Rs. 1,00,000 | Tenure: 12 months
12% FLAT rate → Total interest = Rs. 12,000. Effective annual rate ≈ 21.46% reducing balance
12% REDUCING rate → Total interest = Rs. 6,620.

If a loan states "12% p.a. flat" the true cost is nearly DOUBLE what it appears.

1.3 NBFCs MUST DISCLOSE:
- Interest rate charged (annual percentage)
- Processing and other charges
- Penal interest / charges for late payment
- All terms and conditions of loan in borrower's vernacular language if requested

1.4 NBFC CANNOT:
- Charge interest from date of sanction if funds not yet disbursed
- Capitalise interest on penal charges
- Charge a higher rate than stated in loan agreement without prior written notice
- Use muscle power or intimidation for loan recovery
- Accept security that is disproportionate to loan amount without justification


SECTION 2: RBI DIGITAL LENDING GUIDELINES (September 2022 + subsequent amendments)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Applicable to: All Regulated Entities (REs) and their Lending Service Providers (LSPs) / Digital Lending Apps (DLAs)

2.1 KEY CUSTOMER PROTECTION RULES FOR DIGITAL LOANS

DISBURSEMENT:
- Loan amount must be disbursed DIRECTLY to the borrower's bank account
- No pass-through of funds through the LSP/DLA (to prevent fund siphoning)
- No deduction of fees/charges from disbursed amount without explicit consent

KFS REQUIREMENT:
- KFS must be provided BEFORE loan execution in digital loans
- APR must be prominently disclosed
- Cooling off period must be provided

DATA PRIVACY:
- Lending apps cannot access contacts, photos, media on borrower's device
- Only camera, microphone, location, and identifier permissions are allowed (and only with purpose)
- Data collected cannot be shared with third parties without explicit consent

RED FLAG: Digital loan apps that request access to contacts, photos, or social media are violating RBI data privacy guidelines.

2.2 COLLECTION AND RECOVERY — DIGITAL LENDING
- Recovery agents must follow RBI's code of conduct
- Borrower contact only between 8 AM and 7 PM
- Demand for repayment must specify exact amount and reason
- Threat, harassment, or shaming via social contacts is PROHIBITED
- Calling/messaging borrower's family or employer is PROHIBITED

2.3 GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL FOR DIGITAL LOANS
- Each LSP must have a designated grievance redressal officer
- Complaints must be resolved within 30 days
- RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme applies to digital lenders


SECTION 3: RBI INTEREST RATE ON ADVANCES DIRECTIONS 2016
----------------------------------------------------------
(Relevant to bank loans)

3.1 PRICING FRAMEWORK
Banks must link floating rate retail/MSME loan rates to:
- External Benchmark: RBI Repo Rate (most common), T-Bill rates, or FBIL published rates
- OR MCLR (for certain categories)
The spread charged above the benchmark is determined by borrower's credit profile.

3.2 SPREAD CANNOT CHANGE EXCEPT:
- When borrower's credit profile changes (typically at annual review)
- When RBI regulations explicitly permit

3.3 PREPAYMENT CHARGES — KEY RULES
For floating rate loans to INDIVIDUAL BORROWERS:
- Banks CANNOT charge prepayment penalties (prohibited by RBI)

For fixed rate loans to individual borrowers:
- Banks MAY charge reasonable prepayment penalty

For NBFC loans to individual borrowers:
- Rules vary; NBFCs can set their own policy but must disclose it upfront

RBI RULE: For floating rate loans taken by individuals — any prepayment penalty is a RED FLAG and potential RBI violation.


SECTION 4: LOAN RECOVERY PRACTICES — CODE OF CONDUCT
------------------------------------------------------
RBI has prescribed a code of conduct for loan recovery. Key rules:

MUST DO:
- Provide 60 days notice before initiating recovery proceedings on secured loans (varies by loan type)
- Show original documents and authorisation before visiting borrower
- Conduct recovery only between 7 AM and 7 PM
- Inform borrower of all dues, charges, and outstanding amount in writing

MUST NOT DO:
- Use abusive or threatening language
- Contact family members, employers, or friends of borrower
- Post or publicly display default information (name shaming)
- Threaten legal action that has not actually been initiated
- Misrepresent themselves as court officials or government agents
- Charge for recovery visits without disclosure in loan agreement

RED FLAG CLAUSES IN DOCUMENTS:
- "Borrower authorises lender to contact any person known to borrower for recovery"
- "Cost of recovery shall be borne by borrower" (with no cap specified)
- "Lender may take physical possession without court order" (may be questionable for certain loan types)

=======================================================================
SCORING IMPLICATIONS
=======================================================================

SEVERE RED FLAGS:
- Flat rate stated without equivalent reducing balance rate disclosed: -20 points
- Digital loan with device data access permissions: -15 points
- Prepayment penalty on individual floating rate loan: -15 points (RBI violation)
- Recovery agent can contact friends/family: -15 points

MODERATE RED FLAGS:
- No vernacular language option mentioned: -5 points
- No direct disbursement to borrower account (goes through intermediary): -15 points
- No RBI Ombudsman escalation path mentioned: -5 points

CLARIFICATIONS:
When a loan states "12% flat" — flag it and note that the effective reducing balance rate is approximately 21-22%, and scoring should be based on the effective rate.
