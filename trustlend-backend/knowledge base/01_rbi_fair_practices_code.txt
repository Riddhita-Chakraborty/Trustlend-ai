RBI FAIR PRACTICES CODE FOR LENDERS — FULL GUIDELINES
=======================================================================

SECTION 1: APPLICATIONS FOR LOANS AND THEIR PROCESSING
-------------------------------------------------------
1.1 Loan application forms must be comprehensive and include:
    - All fees and charges payable for processing
    - Whether fees are refundable if application is rejected
    - Pre-payment options available to the borrower
    - Any other matter affecting the borrower's interest
    - Sufficient information to allow borrower to compare with other banks

1.2 Banks and financial institutions must provide written acknowledgement for ALL loan applications received.

1.3 The time frame within which loan applications up to Rs. 2 lakhs will be disposed of must be clearly indicated in the acknowledgement letter.

1.4 Lenders must verify loan applications within a reasonable period of time. If additional documents are required, borrowers must be informed immediately.

1.5 For small borrowers seeking loans up to Rs. 2 lakhs: Lenders MUST communicate in writing the main reason(s) for rejection of loan applications within stipulated time.

RED FLAG: If a lender does not provide written acknowledgement, does not state processing timelines, or does not give reasons for rejection — this violates RBI Fair Practices Code.


SECTION 2: LOAN APPRAISAL AND TERMS/CONDITIONS
-----------------------------------------------
2.1 Lenders must ensure proper assessment of credit applications. They shall NOT use margin and security stipulation as a substitute for due diligence on creditworthiness.

2.2 The lender MUST convey to the borrower:
    - The credit limit sanctioned
    - ALL terms and conditions thereof
    - The borrower's written acceptance of these terms must be on record

2.3 MANDATORY: All terms, conditions, and caveats governing credit facilities MUST be reduced in writing and duly certified by an authorised official.

2.4 MANDATORY: A copy of the loan agreement AND copies of all enclosures quoted in the loan agreement MUST be furnished to the borrower.

2.5 The loan agreement must clearly stipulate credit facilities that are solely at the lender's discretion — including approval/disallowance of drawings beyond sanctioned limits.

RED FLAG: If borrower is not given a complete copy of the loan agreement with all annexures — this violates RBI Fair Practices Code.


SECTION 3: DISBURSEMENT OF LOANS INCLUDING CHANGES IN TERMS AND CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Lenders must ensure timely disbursement of loans in conformity with agreed terms and conditions.

3.2 Lenders MUST give advance notice of any change in terms and conditions including:
    - Interest rates
    - Service charges
    - Any other fees

3.3 CRITICAL RULE: Changes in interest rates and charges must be effected only PROSPECTIVELY — meaning a lender cannot retroactively apply higher rates to past periods.

3.4 Interest should be charged only from the date of ACTUAL disbursement of funds, not from:
    - Date of sanction of loan
    - Date of execution of loan agreement
    - Date the cheque was written (if funds were handed over later)

RED FLAG: Charging interest from sanction date rather than disbursement date is an UNFAIR PRACTICE identified by RBI.


SECTION 4: POST DISBURSEMENT SUPERVISION
-----------------------------------------
4.1 Post-disbursement supervision should be constructive — particularly for loans up to Rs. 2 lakhs.

4.2 Before recalling a loan, accelerating payment, or seeking additional securities, lenders MUST give advance notice to borrowers as specified in the loan agreement, or a reasonable period if no such condition exists.

4.3 Lenders MUST release ALL securities on full repayment of the loan, subject only to any legitimate right of lien for other claims.

4.4 If right of set-off is to be exercised, borrowers must be given full notice with complete particulars.

RED FLAG: Lender demanding additional collateral mid-loan without notice, or refusing to release securities after full repayment, violates this code.


SECTION 5: GENERAL FAIR PRACTICE PROVISIONS
--------------------------------------------
5.1 NON-INTERFERENCE: Lenders must not interfere in the affairs of borrowers except as provided in the loan agreement, unless new undisclosed information has emerged.

5.2 NON-DISCRIMINATION: Lenders must NOT discriminate on grounds of sex, caste, or religion in lending decisions.

5.3 RECOVERY PRACTICES: Lenders must NOT resort to:
    - Persistently bothering borrowers at odd hours
    - Use of muscle power or intimidation for recovery
    - Any form of harassment during recovery

5.4 TRANSFER OF ACCOUNTS: If a borrower requests transfer of their loan account to another bank, the lender's consent or objection must be communicated within 21 days.

5.5 Grievance redressal mechanism must be in place. All disputes must be heard and disposed of at least at the next higher level in the organisation.

RED FLAG: Any harassment during recovery, refusal to allow account transfer without reason, or absence of grievance redressal mechanism is a violation.


SECTION 6: BOARD RESPONSIBILITY
---------------------------------
6.1 The Board of Directors must lay down a clear policy for Fair Practices Code.
6.2 The Board must lay down appropriate grievance redressal mechanism.
6.3 Periodic compliance review of the Fair Practices Code must be submitted to the Board.

=======================================================================
SCORING IMPLICATIONS FOR LOAN DOCUMENT ANALYSIS
=======================================================================

INSTANT RED FLAGS (Severe — deduct heavily from fairness score):
- Interest charged from sanction date, not disbursement date
- No written acknowledgement process described
- No copy of agreement promised to borrower
- Retroactive changes to interest rates mentioned
- Aggressive recovery/intimidation clauses

MODERATE CONCERNS (deduct moderately):
- No grievance redressal mechanism described
- No pre-payment information provided
- Vague or absent timeline disclosures
- No written rejection reason policy

GOOD SIGNS (neutral or positive for fairness):
- Clear timeline for loan processing stated
- Written acknowledgement process described
- Pre-payment options clearly disclosed
- Grievance officer contact provided
