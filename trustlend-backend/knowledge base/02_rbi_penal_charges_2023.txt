RBI CIRCULAR: FAIR LENDING PRACTICE — PENAL CHARGES IN LOAN ACCOUNTS
=======================================================================

BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE
-----------------------
The RBI observed widespread misuse of penal interest by lenders:
- Banks were using penal charges as a REVENUE ENHANCEMENT TOOL rather than as a deterrent for credit discipline
- Penal interest was being added ON TOP OF the normal interest rate, causing compounding
- Practices were inconsistent and non-transparent across institutions
- This was causing customer grievances and was not in the spirit of fairness

This circular FUNDAMENTALLY CHANGES how penalties must be structured in all new Indian loan agreements effective from 2024.


KEY RULE 1: PENAL INTEREST IS ABOLISHED — REPLACED BY PENAL CHARGES
----------------------------------------------------------------------
OLD PRACTICE (UNFAIR): Lenders added an extra interest rate (e.g., "2% penal interest per month") ON TOP of the normal contracted interest rate for defaults.

NEW RULE (FAIR): Penalty for default must be levied as a FLAT "PENAL CHARGE" — not as an additional interest rate percentage.

CRITICAL DISTINCTION:
- Penal charges are a fixed charge on the defaulted/overdue amount only
- Penal charges CANNOT be compounded (no interest on the penal charge itself)
- Normal contracted interest continues to accrue on the outstanding loan balance
- Penal charges apply ONLY on the amount that is actually overdue/in default

RED FLAG IN LOAN DOCUMENTS: Any clause that says "X% per month penal interest will be added to the rate of interest" OR "interest rate will increase by X% on default" is likely NON-COMPLIANT with RBI 2023 circular.


KEY RULE 2: PENAL CHARGES MUST BE REASONABLE AND PROPORTIONATE
----------------------------------------------------------------
The RBI explicitly states:
- Penal charges must be REASONABLE and JUSTIFIED
- Charges must be PROPORTIONATE to the nature and severity of the breach
- Penal charges must NOT be used as a revenue tool
- Different categories of loans may have different penal charge structures

WHAT IS UNREASONABLE (RED FLAGS):
- Flat penal charges that are a large percentage of the loan amount (e.g., 5%+ of total outstanding)
- Penal charges triggered by minor technical defaults (e.g., delay of 1 day)
- Penal charges with no cap
- Charges that reset monthly and accumulate rapidly


KEY RULE 3: MANDATORY DISCLOSURE OF PENAL CHARGES
---------------------------------------------------
ALL of the following must be disclosed to borrowers:
1. Penal charges must be disclosed in the LOAN AGREEMENT itself
2. Penal charges must be disclosed in the Key Fact Statement (KFS)
3. Penal charges must be listed on the lender's WEBSITE under "Interest Rates and Service Charges"
4. Whenever a payment reminder is sent, penal charges applicable must be communicated

RED FLAG: If penal charges are NOT listed in the loan agreement, KFS, or are buried in fine print — this is a disclosure violation.


KEY RULE 4: NO CAPITALIZATION OF PENAL CHARGES
------------------------------------------------
Penal charges CANNOT be capitalized. This means:
- Unpaid penal charges cannot be added to the loan principal
- No further interest can accrue ON the penal charges
- Only the normal contracted interest continues on the outstanding balance
- Penal charges accrue only on the defaulted portion, not on the entire outstanding loan

EXAMPLE OF UNFAIR PRACTICE (NOW PROHIBITED):
Loan outstanding: Rs. 10,00,000
Defaulted EMI: Rs. 25,000
Wrong (old) way: Add 2% penal interest on Rs. 10,00,000 = Rs. 20,000/month penal interest (compounding monthly)
Correct (new) way: Fixed penal charge on Rs. 25,000 defaulted amount only, no compounding


KEY RULE 5: PRIOR COMMUNICATION REQUIREMENT
--------------------------------------------
Before levying penal charges, lenders must:
- Send a payment reminder
- Communicate the applicable penal charges in that reminder
- Allow a reasonable time for borrower to rectify the default


WHAT THESE RULES DO NOT COVER
-------------------------------
These penal charge rules do NOT apply to:
- Credit cards (governed by separate product-specific directions)
- External Commercial Borrowings (ECBs)
- Trade Credits
- Structured Obligations

=======================================================================
SCORING IMPLICATIONS FOR LOAN DOCUMENT ANALYSIS
=======================================================================

SEVERE RED FLAGS (deduct 25-40 points from fairness score):
- Penal interest stated as "X% per month added to interest rate" (old-style, now prohibited)
- Compounding of penal interest mentioned
- Penal charges applicable on ENTIRE outstanding balance, not just defaulted amount
- No disclosure of penal charges in loan agreement

MODERATE RED FLAGS (deduct 10-20 points):
- Penal charges disclosed only in fine print / schedule, not highlighted
- Penal charges appear disproportionately high (e.g., 5%+ per month on overdue amount)
- No mention of when penal charges will be waived/stopped
- Multiple overlapping penalty triggers

GOOD SIGNS:
- Penal charges stated as flat amounts per instance or per month on overdue only
- Penal charges clearly tabulated in a schedule
- Explicit statement that charges are not compounded
- Charges proportionate (e.g., Rs. 500-2000 per default event)
