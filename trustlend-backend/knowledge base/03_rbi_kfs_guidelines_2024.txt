RBI KEY FACT STATEMENT (KFS) — MANDATORY LOAN DISCLOSURE FRAMEWORK
=======================================================================

WHAT IS A KEY FACT STATEMENT (KFS)?
-------------------------------------
A KFS is a standardised, mandatory summary document that lenders MUST provide to borrowers BEFORE the loan agreement is executed. It contains all essential loan information in simple language.

PURPOSE: Eliminate information asymmetry. A borrower with a KFS can compare loans across lenders accurately and make an informed decision.

MANDATORY FROM OCTOBER 1, 2024:
All new retail and MSME term loans (including fresh loans to existing customers) must have a KFS. No exceptions.

SCOPE: Applies to personal loans, home loans, auto loans, education loans, business loans for retail and MSME borrowers.
EXCLUDED: Credit cards (separate framework applies).


WHAT MUST A KFS CONTAIN — MANDATORY FIELDS
--------------------------------------------
1. LOAN IDENTIFICATION
   - Loan type and product name
   - Loan account number (if applicable)
   - Lender name and branch

2. LOAN AMOUNT AND TENURE
   - Total loan amount sanctioned
   - Total loan tenure (in months/years)
   - Moratorium period, if any

3. INTEREST RATE (COMPLETE DISCLOSURE)
   - Nature of rate: Fixed / Floating / Hybrid
   - If fixed: The rate in % per annum
   - If floating: Benchmark rate used (Repo rate, MCLR, etc.) + spread
   - Reset frequency for floating rates
   - Floor and cap on interest rate, if applicable

4. ANNUAL PERCENTAGE RATE (APR) — CRITICAL FIELD
   - APR = Total annual cost of credit to borrower
   - APR includes: Interest rate + ALL processing fees + ALL other charges
   - APR includes: Third-party charges (insurance premiums, legal fees, valuation charges)
   - APR must be expressed as a SINGLE, all-inclusive percentage
   - CHARGES NOT IN KFS CANNOT BE LEVIED on the borrower without explicit consent

5. EMI / REPAYMENT SCHEDULE
   - EMI amount
   - Total number of EMIs
   - Amortization table showing breakup of principal and interest

6. ALL FEES AND CHARGES (COMPLETE LIST — NO HIDDEN CHARGES ALLOWED)
   - Processing fees (one-time)
   - Documentation charges
   - Stamp duty
   - Insurance premium (if mandatory)
   - Valuation/legal charges
   - Prepayment/foreclosure charges
   - Late payment charges / penal charges (must match Penal Charges circular)
   - Any other charges whatsoever

7. SECURITY / COLLATERAL
   - Nature of collateral required
   - Conditions for release of security

8. COOLING-OFF / LOOK-UP PERIOD
   - Minimum 1 day (Board of RE determines actual period)
   - During this period, borrower can exit the loan without any penalty
   - This is a borrower's RIGHT under the 2024 guidelines

9. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
   - Name and contact of designated Grievance Officer
   - Escalation path and timeline
   - RBI Ombudsman details


CRITICAL RULES ABOUT THE KFS
------------------------------
RULE 1 — KFS VALIDITY PERIOD:
The lender cannot change the terms stated in the KFS during its validity period. The borrower must have a stable, comparable offer to evaluate.

RULE 2 — BORROWER ACKNOWLEDGEMENT:
The borrower's written acknowledgement of having received and understood the KFS must be obtained BEFORE the loan agreement is signed.

RULE 3 — KFS IN LOAN AGREEMENT:
The KFS must be included as a SUMMARY BOX within the loan agreement itself. It cannot be a separate document that gets lost.

RULE 4 — NO UNDISCLOSED CHARGES:
Any fee, charge, or cost NOT mentioned in the KFS CANNOT be levied on the borrower without their explicit, specific consent. This is a strong consumer protection rule.

RULE 5 — AMENDMENTS TO LOAN TERMS:
After the loan is disbursed, if any terms change, the lender must get explicit borrower consent. Unilateral changes to loan terms are prohibited.

RULE 6 — DIGITAL LENDING:
All digital lending platforms are also covered. Digital lenders must provide KFS before disbursement.


WHAT HAPPENS IF KFS IS MISSING OR INCOMPLETE?
-----------------------------------------------
- Non-compliance is subject to RBI supervisory action
- RBI can impose fines, issue warnings, restrict lending activities
- Borrower can challenge hidden charges not disclosed in KFS
- Charges levied without KFS disclosure can be recovered by borrower

=======================================================================
SCORING IMPLICATIONS FOR LOAN DOCUMENT ANALYSIS
=======================================================================

SEVERE RED FLAGS (deduct 30-40 points):
- No KFS or KFS summary box present in the loan document
- APR not disclosed (only interest rate given, hiding fees)
- Charges present in loan that are not listed in any schedule
- "Subject to change without notice" language for charges

MODERATE RED FLAGS (deduct 15-25 points):
- Fees listed but APR not calculated/stated
- Insurance premium listed as mandatory but not included in cost calculation
- No grievance officer contact provided
- Cooling-off period not mentioned

GOOD SIGNS:
- KFS box visible at start of loan agreement
- APR clearly stated including all third-party charges
- Complete fee schedule provided upfront
- Cooling-off period and grievance mechanism stated
- Explicit statement: "No charges other than listed above shall be levied"
